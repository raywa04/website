# Exploring Emotional Perception: My Journey as an Undergraduate Research Assistant at UT Dallas

## Introduction to UT Dallas

My undergraduate research assistantship at the University of Texas at Dallas (UT Dallas) marked an enriching chapter in my academic journey. Working within the Computer and Electrical Engineering department in Richardson, TX, I had the opportunity to delve into the fascinating world of emotional perception research.

## Analyzing Conversational Data

One of the pivotal aspects of my role was analyzing vast amounts of conversational data, totaling over 500 hours. Through meticulous examination, I identified key patterns and trends in emotional content perception. This process provided valuable insights into the complexities of human emotions and paved the way for further exploration in the field.

## Collaborative Research Endeavors

At UT Dallas, collaboration was at the core of our research efforts. I had the privilege of engaging with a dedicated team of researchers to develop and optimize deep learning algorithms using TensorFlow. By leveraging our collective expertise and pooling our resources, we aimed to push the boundaries of emotional perception research and contribute to advancements in the field.

## Spearheading Innovative Research

One of the highlights of my research assistantship was spearheading initiatives focused on advancing methods and models for recognizing human verbal and non-verbal behaviors. Through a combination of theoretical exploration and practical experimentation, we sought to refine existing techniques and develop novel approaches to enhance our understanding of emotional perception.
